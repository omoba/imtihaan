The Cost of Cheating

It was a bright Tuesday morning at Al-Noor Elementary School. The classroom buzzed with excitement as Teacher Fatima announced, "Tomorrow, we'll have a math competition. The top student will get a special prize!"

Amir's eyes widened. "A prize? I want to win, InshaAllah!" he whispered to his friend Hasan.

That evening, at home, Amir sat at his desk with his math notebook open. The questions were tricky. "Hmm," he muttered. "If I could just look at Hasan's answers tomorrow, no one would know."

Amirah, his twin sister, overheard him. "Amir! That's cheating," she said firmly. "You can't do that."
Amir shrugged. "It's just once. It won't hurt anyone."
Amirah frowned. "But it will displease Allah."
Amir rolled his eyes. "Nothing bad will happen. You'll see."

The next day, during the competition, Amir peeked at Hasan's paper and copied a few answers. When Teacher Fatima announced the results, Amir had the highest score. Everyone clapped.

"Alhamdulillah!" Amir shouted proudly. He whispered to Amirah, "See? No one got hurt."
Amirah sighed. "Just because nothing bad happened doesn't make it right."

That evening, Baba overheard them. "Amir," he said gently, "Do you know what the Prophet ﷺ said about cheating?"
Amir looked up. Baba recited:

"The Messenger of Allah ﷺ passed by a man who was selling food. He put his hand in it and saw that there was something wrong with it. The Prophet ﷺ said, 'He is not one of us who cheats.'" (Sahih Muslim)

Baba continued, "Cheating means hiding the truth. Even if no one sees it, Allah does. And when we cheat, we lose His blessings."
Amir felt a little uneasy but didn't say anything.

Days passed. Amir began to cheat in small ways—copying homework answers, taking shortcuts during chores, and pretending to finish his prayers quickly. Each time, nothing bad seemed to happen.

One afternoon, during science class, Amir whispered to Hasan, "Let me copy your experiment notes. We'll finish faster." Hasan hesitated but agreed. They both got good grades again.

Amir smirked. "See? Cheating helps sometimes."

Amirah shook her head. "Maybe now, but one day, you'll regret it, Amir."

The following week, Teacher Fatima announced a new group project. Amir was excited. "I'll lead our group!" he said. But instead of researching properly, he copied an entire report from a website and added his name to it.

On presentation day, the teacher listened quietly. When Amir finished, she said calmly, "Amir, can you explain how this experiment works?"

Amir froze. He didn't understand what he had copied. His classmates stared. Hasan whispered, "Oh no…"

Teacher Fatima sighed. "Amir, I checked. This report was copied from the internet. You cheated."

Amir's face turned red. "I'm sorry," he said softly.

After class, Baba was called in. Amir admitted everything, tears rolling down his cheeks. "I thought it didn't hurt anyone," he sobbed. "But now I feel ashamed."

Baba hugged him. "My son, Allah gives us many chances to do right. The worst part of cheating isn't getting caught—it's losing Allah's pleasure."

Amir nodded, wiping his tears. "I'll never cheat again, InshaAllah. I want Allah to be pleased with me."

From that day, Amir worked honestly, even when it was hard. He began to feel peaceful knowing Allah was pleased with his efforts.

Whenever he saw someone tempted to cheat, he would remind them, "The Prophet ﷺ said, 'He is not one of us who cheats.'"

And he meant it with all his heart.
